Corin Cartwright: How To Prepare to Conduct an Interview

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How do you prepare for an interview ...

Work time Pre interview:30 minutes to 1 hour
Peace of mind and feeling prepared: 100%

Are you an up coming Journalist or student and have to conduct an interview for the first time, then you've come to the right place!

  • A general Idea on what your story will be on

  • Set up an interview
  • come prepared with open ended questions
  • be a good listener
  • stay focused but be willing to hear more than what you want to hear
  • stay calm
  • Bring any equipment you would need (camera, microphone, recorder, ect)

  1. Educate yourself

    interview photo from Flikr

    Before you can even think about looking for an interviewee you must first figure out what you want your story to be on, and then educate yourself on that topic. Figure out what message you want to share and then research everything you can on that subject.

  2. Get someone to interview

    Once you know what you want to report on find someone who is well versed and knowlegeble in what you want to you want to report on. Make sure this person is relevant to your subject and can give you the information you need for your story. Make sure that when you ask this individual(s) for an interview make sure you are very poliet, if someone is willing to be interviewed by you they are doing you a favor.

    open note book

  3. Prepare your questions

    Before you can conduct your interview make sure you pepare your questions ahead of time. Make your questions relevent to your subject while also having them be open ended. Make sure that you are writing questions that you aren't looking for a very specific answer, give your interviewee the room to answer how they want, not how you want them to.

  4. Be a good LISTENER

    This step is esspecially important that you only talk when you really need to, most people tend to really enjoy talking about things they know about so if you are talking too much you are denying yourself infomation. It is also your responsibility to set the mood of your interview, sometimes the subject of your story isn't a happy and its possible that your story subject might be a sensitive subject for your interviewee. So being a good listener is a great skill to use to get infomation you have have not known you needed while also keeping your interviewee comfortable enough to speak.

  5. Stay calm

    For most people conduction an interview can be very stressful and scary but the main thing to remember is to stay calm. Its good to remember that you are simpily having a normal conversation with another person not a wild animal who will bite your head off. If you have followed the previous steps you should be totally prepared for your interview so all you have to do is stay calm and have a simple conversation with your inverviewee.
